# Hiring — Team Scaling

## When to Hire

### Phase 1: Solo ($0-$30K/mo)
- You do everything
- No hire yet
- Focus: deliver excellent service, build reputation

### Phase 2: First Hire ($30K-$60K/mo)
- 1 delivery person
- You: sales + strategy + key clients
- Role: AI implementation specialist

### Phase 3: Growth ($60K-$100K/mo)
- 2-3 delivery + 1 VA
- You: sales + CEO relationships
- Role: hire from your trainees/clients

### Phase 4: Scale ($100K-$200K/mo)
- 5 team members
- You: vision + partnerships
- Role: ops manager to run delivery

### Phase 5: Enterprise ($200K+/mo)
- Full team, departments
- You: strategy + big accounts
- Role: sales closer, department leads

---

## Who to Hire First

### Option 1: AI Implementation Specialist
**When:** Need help delivering to 3+ clients
**What they do:** Build automations, dashboards, train teams
**Profile:** Technical but not necessarily a coder
**Pay:** $50K-$80K/year or $40-$60/hr contractor

### Option 2: Virtual Assistant
**When:** Swamped with admin
**What they do:** Scheduling, invoices, follow-ups, research
**Profile:** Organized, good English, proactive
**Pay:** $25-$40/hr (VA) or $2K-$4K/mo (part-time)

### Option 3: Sales/Closers
**When:** Have leads but can't close
**What they do:** Discovery calls, follow-ups, proposals
**Profile:** Consultative sales experience
**Pay:** Base + commission (20% of deal)

## Hiring Sources

### Best Sources
1. **Your trainees/clients** — Already know your system
2. **LinkedIn** — Post you're hiring
3. **Upwork/Fiverr** — Contractors for specific skills
4. **Referrals** — Team recommends people
5. **Remote job boards** — We Work Remotely, Remote OK

### What to Look For
- Problem-solving attitude
- Learning agility (AI changes fast)
- Communication skills
- Ownership mentality
- Coachable

---

## Interview Process

### Stage 1: Application
- Simple form: name, email, LinkedIn, why interested
- Filter: must have relevant experience

### Stage 2: 15-min Call
- Quick fit check
- Explain role
- 2-3 questions about background

### Stage 3: Task Assignment
- Give a paid trial task
- Example: "Build a simple Zapier automation"
- Pay $100-$200 for 1-2 hours

### Stage 4: Final Interview
- Discuss task results
- Deep dive on experience
- Cultural fit

### Stage 5: Offer
- Trial period (1-3 months)
- Clear expectations
- Regular check-ins

## Onboarding New Hires

### Week 1: Learn Your System
- Read playbook (this skill!)
- Watch training videos
- Shadow your client calls
- Set up all tools

### Week 2: Assist
- Join calls as observer
- Help with simple tasks
- Start using your systems
- Weekly 1:1 check-in

### Week 3: Lead Small Pieces
- Take on one client deliverable
- Full support available
- Document what works

### Week 4: Independent
- Own client deliverable
- Full responsibility
- Regular check-ins

---

## Managing Contractors

### Communication
- Weekly video call (30 min)
- Daily Slack check-in
- Clear task definitions

### Expectations
- Deliverables, not hours
- Set clear deadlines
- Weekly summary of what done

### Payment
- Net 15 or Net 30
- Track hours if hourly
- Invoice prompt

---

## When to Let Go

### Warning Signs
- Missed deadlines repeatedly
- Quality issues
- Communication problems
- Negative attitude
- Not learning/growing

### Process
1. First warning (document)
2. Second warning (document)
3. Final warning (document)
4. Release with notice

### Always
- Be fair and clear
- Give feedback
- Pay what owed
- Wish them well

## John Cheney's Hiring Advice

> "My first employees were people who had taken my courses and trainings. They already understood my methodology, my tools, and my philosophy. I didn't have to train them from scratch."

**Key insight:** If you're doing group trainings for clients, you're simultaneously building a pipeline of potential hires who already know your system.
