# Crisis — Problem Protocols

## Client Complaints

### "You're Not Delivering Value"

**Immediate Response (within 4 hours):**
```
Hey [Name], I hear you and I take this seriously. Can we hop on a call this week to understand what's not working? I want to make this right.
```

**On the Call:**
1. Listen fully (don't defend)
2. Ask: "What would success look like for you?"
3. Acknowledge their frustration
4. Agree on specific next steps

**Follow Up:**
- Send summary of what you agreed
- Execute on timeline
- Check in within 3 days

---

### "The Dashboard/Breakdown Isn't Working"

**Immediate (within 2 hours):**
```
Got it, I'm on it. Can you send me a screenshot or describe what specifically isn't working?
```

**Fix:**
1. Reproduce the issue
2. Fix or escalate to your developer
3. Test the fix
4. Confirm with client

**Prevent:**
- Build in testing time
- Have backup options ready
- Document known issues

---

### "This Is Taking Too Long"

**Response:**
```
I hear you. We've been building the foundation, which takes time. Let me show you what we've accomplished and what's coming next. [Show progress]
```

**Then:**
- Adjust timeline if reasonable
- Add more frequent check-ins
- Show visible progress more often

## Scope Creep

### How to Handle

**Identify scope creep:**
- "While you're at it, can you also..."
- Requests outside original agreement
- Increasing frequency of requests

**Response:**
```
I'd love to help with that. That would be an additional [hours/amount] because it's outside our current scope. Want me to add it to next month's plan?
```

**Document:**
- Track all out-of-scope requests
- Review at monthly meeting
- Discuss in strategy calls

## Non-Payment

### If Payment is Late

**Day 1-7 (due date + 7):**
- No action (grace period)

**Day 8:**
```
Hi [Name], just a friendly reminder that invoice #[X] is now [X] days past due. Let me know if there's anything I can help with.
```

**Day 15:**
```
Hi [Name], Invoice #[X] is now 15 days overdue. Is everything okay? Please let me know when we can expect payment.
```

**Day 30:**
```
Hi [Name], Unfortunately, services will be paused until payment is received. Please confirm when we can expect payment to resume.
```

### If They Stop Responding

**Step 1:** Try all channels (email, phone, text, LinkedIn)
**Step 2:** Send formal letter (email + certified mail)
**Step 3:** Consider small claims court (if significant)
**Step 4:** Move on and learn

## Technical Failures

### Server/Downtime Issues

**Immediate:**
- Acknowledge the issue publicly
- Give timeline for fix
- Update every 30 minutes

**Root Cause:**
- Fix the issue
- Document what happened
- Prevent recurrence

**Client Communication:**
```
We're experiencing a brief issue with [system]. Our team is working on it - expected resolution by [time]. I'll update you when it's fixed.
```

### Data Loss

**Immediate:**
- Assess what was lost
- Restore from backups if possible
- Notify client immediately

**Resolution:**
- Explain what happened
- Timeline for recovery
- Compensation (discount on next month)
- Prevention measures

---

## Churn Prevention

### Warning Signs
- Reduced engagement
- Fewer responses
- Complaints increasing
- Check-ins refused

### Intervention
- Reach out proactively
- Ask: "Is everything okay?"
- Offer to adjust scope
- Fix problems fast

### If They Want to Cancel

**Response:**
```
I understand. Before we finalize, can I ask - what could we have done differently? I'd also like to offer a 90-day transition plan where I [X] so you're not left hanging.
```

**Then:**
- Ask for feedback (for your playbook)
- Ask for referral
- Wish them well
- Document lessons learned

---

## Personal Burnout

### Signs
- Dreading client calls
- Missing deadlines
- Quality dropping
- No energy for new business

### Protocol
1. **Acknowledge** — It's normal, happens to everyone
2. **Rest** — Take a day or weekend off
3. **Delegate** — Push work to next week
4. **Reassess** — Is workload sustainable?
5. **Plan** — Hire help or adjust capacity

---

## You Don't Know the Answer

### When Client Asks Something You Don't Know

**Immediate:**
"Great question. Let me research that and get back to you by [specific time]."

**Follow Through:**
- Actually research
- Get back with answer
- Or: "I found this, but also recommend [expert] for deeper dive"

**Why It's Okay:**
- You're the strategist, not the coder
- Part of value is finding answers
- Models like ChatGPT/Claude can help

## Competitive Pressure

### If Client Gets Other Proposals

**Response:**
```
I hear you. Happy to compare our approach to [competitor]. Here's what makes us different: [key differentiators]. What specifically are they offering that we aren't?
```

**Then:**
- Address concerns directly
- Offer to adjust proposal
- Know when to walk away

## Emergency Protocol Summary

| Situation | Response Time | First Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| System down | Immediate | Acknowledge + fix |
| Client complaint | 4 hours | Listen + schedule call |
| Non-payment | 7 days | Friendly reminder |
| Churn risk | Immediate | Reach out + fix |
| Technical issue | 2 hours | Fix or escalate |
| You don't know | 24 hours | Research + respond |

## Documentation

### Always Document
- What went wrong
- How you fixed it
- What you'll do differently
- Updates to playbook

### This Makes You Better
- Each crisis is a learning opportunity
- Update your systems
- Improve your playbook
- Get stronger over time
