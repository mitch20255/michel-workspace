# Quebec Market Adaptation

## Why Quebec is Different

### Business Culture
- More relationship-driven than US
- Face-to-face preferred over email/cold outreach
- French-English bilingual usually expected
- Smaller business community (everyone knows everyone)
- Trust takes longer but is stronger once built

### Market Size
- Population: 8.5M
- GDP: ~$450B CAD
- SMBs: ~500K businesses
- Target: $10M-$50M revenue firms (smaller pool than US)

### Language
- French is dominant (except Montreal)
- English needed for Montreal business
- Marketing in French for outside Montreal
- Some Montreal businesses prefer English

## Quebec Sales Adaptation

### Relationship Building
- More initial touchpoints before sale
- Coffee/meal meetings valued
- Reference calls more important
- Trust-based selling

### Cultural Notes
- Don't be too "salesy"
- Be authentic, not polished
- Show expertise through questions
- Accept that sales cycle is longer

### French Sales Call Adaptation
- Can do in French or English (client preference)
- More formal initial contact
- Use "vous" not "tu" until relationship established
- Slower pace, more relationship time

## Quebec Operations

### Invoicing (Taxes)
- GST (5%): Federal
- QST (9.975%): Provincial
- Must register if revenue >$30K/year
- Must collect and remit both

### Quebec Legal
- Civil law (different from common law)
- Consider French-language contracts
- Lawyer for contract review recommended
- OQLF requirements for French labels

### Banking
- Most major banks: RBC, TD, BMO, Desjardins
- Desjardins popular with Quebec businesses
- Smaller local banks exist

## Summary

Quebec is a smaller market but with real opportunity:
- Less competition than US
- Same problems (no CTO, need AI)
- Language adds complexity but creates differentiation
- Montreal is bilingual-friendly
- Relationship-focused selling works

Start with Montreal (bilingual), then expand French coverage as you grow.
