# Sales — Closing & Proposals

## Proposal Structure

### Option 1: Simple Email Proposal
For warm leads or referral calls:

```
Subject: Summary of Our Conversation

Hi [Name],

Great speaking with you today. Here's what we discussed:

[Summary of their challenges and goals]

Here's what I recommend:
[Selected tier and what's included]

Investment: $[X]/month or $[X] one-time

Next steps:
1. Review this summary
2. Let me know if you have questions
3. If ready to move forward, I'll send the contract

Looking forward to working together.

Best,
Michel
[Phone]
[Calendar link]
```

### Option 2: Formal Proposal Document
For larger deals or enterprise:

**Sections:**
1. Executive Summary (1 paragraph)
2. Current State Assessment
3. Proposed Solution (aligned to their goals)
4. Deliverables & Timeline
5. Investment
6. Terms & Next Steps
7. Appendix (team, methodology, case studies)

## Contract Essentials

### Key Clauses

**Scope Definition:**
```
The Services include:
- [List specific deliverables]
- [Hours per month or project scope]
- [What's NOT included]

Excluded from Services:
- Hardware procurement
- Third-party software licenses
- Custom development beyond defined scope
```

**Payment Terms:**
```
Payment due within [15/30] days of invoice.
Late payments: [X]% per month interest.
Annual contract: [X]% discount.
```

**Termination:**
```
Either party may terminate with [30] days written notice.
Early termination fee: [X]% of remaining contract value.
```

**Confidentiality:**
```
All client data and information remains confidential.
Non-disclosure of proprietary methods and processes.
```

## Objection Handling

### "Too Expensive"
**Response 1 (ROI framing):**
"This is less than the cost of one full-time employee. You're getting AI capabilities that would take a team years to develop. Let me show you the math."

**Response 2 (Breaking it down):**
"$15K/month is about $500/day. How much revenue does your business generate per day? If we help you close one extra deal per month, we're already paid for."

**Response 3 (Scope reduction):**
"What if we started with Growth at $10K/month and expanded as you see results? No long-term commitment."

### "Need to Talk to My Partner"
**Response:**
"Absolutely. Who else is involved? I'd recommend we get them on a quick call so we're all aligned. I have [time] or [time] available this week."

### "We Have a Small Budget"
**Response:**
"Understood. What budget range are you working with? We have options starting at $5K one-time for an AI jumpstart, which gives you the assessment and roadmap to move forward."

### "Not Ready to Commit"
**Response:**
"Completely fair. What would need to change for you to feel ready? Let's set a date 30/60/90 days out and reassess. In the meantime, here's some resources to get started."

### "Send Me a Proposal First"
**Response:**
"Great. Can I ask - what specifically are you most interested in? I want to make sure the proposal addresses what matters most to you."

## Closing Techniques

### The Referral Close (For Referral Leads)
"Tell me about your business. Where do you think I could help? Great, here's how we sign up."

(Note: They already want it. Don't over-explain.)

### The Summary Close
"So to summarize: [repeat key points]. Are you ready to get started?"

### The Timeline Close
"Most companies want to start as soon as possible to get ahead. Would you prefer to start this week or next?"

### The Alternative Close
"Would you prefer the Growth package at $10K or the Scale package at $15K?"

## Contract Workflow

### Step 1: Verbal Agreement
Get verbal commitment on the call or same day.

### Step 2: Send Contract (Same Day)
- Use template (see operations.md)
- Include scope, timeline, investment
- Send via email with PDF

### Step 3: Follow Up (24 hours)
"Hey [Name], wanted to make sure you received the contract. Any questions?"

### Step 4: Invoice (Upon Signature)
Send invoice with payment terms.

### Step 5: Onboarding (Upon Payment)
Schedule onboarding call. See delivery-month1.md.

## Follow-Up Sequences

### If No Response After Proposal
- Day 1: Send proposal
- Day 3: "Hey [Name], just checking if you had a chance to review the proposal?"
- Day 7: "I understand things are busy. Let me know if you have any questions."
- Day 14: "Following up on our proposal. Happy to adjust if needed. Let's connect."
- Day 21: "Just checking in one more time. Is this still a priority?"

### If They Say They're Going to Think About It
- Week 1: Send relevant case study
- Week 2: Send industry article about AI trends
- Week 3: "Hey [Name], any updates on the decision? Happy to hop on a call."

### If They Say No
- "I appreciate you letting me know. Would you mind if I check in again in 3 months?"
- Ask for referral: "Do you know any other business owners who might benefit?"
